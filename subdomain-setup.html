Subdomain Setup Guide

Configure a subdomain for sending emails through Pathway. Using a subdomain is recommended if you already have email services on your root domain.

Great choice!

Using a subdomain is recommended if you already have email services (like Google Workspace or Microsoft 365) on your root domain. This prevents conflicts with your existing email routing.

Prerequisites

Domain ownership

You must own or have administrative access to the parent domain you want to configure.

DNS access

You need the ability to add TXT and CNAME records to your domain's DNS settings.

Pathway account

An active Pathway subscription with admin privileges.

Add Your Subdomain

Follow these steps to add your subdomain to Pathway. Once added, you'll receive the DNS records needed to complete the setup.

1

Open Settings

Locate the Settings icon (gear icon) in the sidebar on the left side of your screen. You'll find it near the bottom of the sidebar, just above the logout button. Click on it to open the Settings menu.

The Settings icon is highlighted in red in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar.

2

Navigate to Email Settings

Once in Settings, follow this navigation path to reach the Email Domains section:

  1. Click on the Practice tab in the top navigation bar
  2. Select the Communication tab from the secondary menu
  3. Click on the Email sub-tab to view email settings

The numbered boxes show the navigation sequence: (1) Practice tab, (2) Communication, (3) Email, and (4) the Add button.

3

Click "Add" to add your subdomain

In the Added Domains section, click the Add button (highlighted as step 4 in the screenshot above) to open the domain registration dialog.

4

Enter your subdomain

Enter your full subdomain (e.g., mail.yourdomain.com) and click Register domain.

The dialog highlights where to enter your subdomain and the Register domain button to confirm.

Note: Enter the complete subdomain including the parent domain (e.g., mail.example.com, not just mail).

5

Review your DNS records

After registering your subdomain, you'll be taken to the domain configuration page. Here you'll see all the DNS records that need to be added to your domain provider. Your subdomain will show a pending status until verification is complete.

The domain configuration page displays all required DNS records. The highlighted areas show: (1) Hostname - the DNS record name, (2) Value - the record content to copy, and (3) Verify button - click after adding records.

This page displays four types of records:

  • TXT Verification Record — Proves you own this subdomain. Add this first.
  • TXT SPF Record — Authorizes Pathway to send emails on your behalf.
  • TXT DKIM Record — Adds a digital signature to authenticate your emails.
  • TXT DMARC Record — Protects your subdomain from email spoofing.

Scroll down to see the remaining DNS records — SPF, DKIM, and DMARC for your subdomain:

These records authorize sending from your subdomain (SPF), add a cryptographic signature (DKIM), and define your spoofing policy (DMARC). Each section shows the Record Type, Hostname, and Value you'll need to configure at your DNS provider.

Tip: Use the copy button next to each value to copy it to your clipboard. Keep this page open while you configure your DNS settings.

6

Add DNS records to your domain provider

Log in to your domain registrar or DNS provider (e.g., Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap) and add each of the DNS records shown in Pathway. You'll need to create TXT records for verification, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.

Follow these steps in your DNS provider:

1

Navigate to DNS Records

In your DNS provider, find the section to add or manage DNS records.

2

Select "TXT" as the record type

All four records (verification, SPF, DKIM, DMARC) use TXT type.

3

Enter the Name (hostname)

For subdomain records, enter the subdomain prefix (e.g., mail for mail.yourdomain.com). For DKIM, combine with the subdomain (e.g., treatmentpath._domainkey.mail).

4

Paste the Content (value)

Copy the Value from Pathway and paste it into the Content field.

5

Click "Save" to add the record

Once you've entered all the information, save the DNS record. Repeat this process for each of the four records.

Here are examples of adding DNS records for a subdomain in Cloudflare:

Adding the Verification Record:

For the verification record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter your subdomain (e.g., app), (4) Paste the verification value, and (5) Click Save.

Adding the SPF Record:

For the SPF record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter your subdomain (e.g., app), (4) Paste the SPF value, and (5) Click Save.

Adding the DKIM Record:

For the DKIM record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter treatmentpath._domainkey.app (combining the DKIM selector with your subdomain), (4) Paste the DKIM value, and (5) Click Save.

Adding the DMARC Record:

For the DMARC record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter _dmarc.app (combining _dmarc with your subdomain), (4) Paste the DMARC value, and (5) Click Save.

Important: Subdomain hostnames

When adding records for a subdomain, some DNS providers automatically append your root domain. Double-check that the final hostname matches exactly what Pathway shows.

Recommended: Use the shortest TTL available

We recommend setting TTL (Time To Live) to the shortest value available — typically 60 seconds (1 minute) or 300 seconds (5 minutes). A shorter TTL allows DNS changes to propagate faster, making verification and troubleshooting quicker.

7

Verify your DNS records

After adding all DNS records, return to Pathway and click the Verify button next to each record. DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to propagate, so if verification fails initially, wait and try again later.

Once all your DNS records are properly configured, the verification page will show green checkmarks next to each record type (Verification Record, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC).

Scroll down to see the full verification status for SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. Each section shows the record type, hostname, and value that was verified.

What each status means:

✓ Verified — Record found and correct
⏳ Pending — Waiting for DNS propagation

All verified?

Once all records show as verified, your subdomain is ready! You can now send emails from your custom subdomain through Pathway.

8

Set up Inbound Parse for receiving messages (Optional)

If you want to receive email replies from patients through Pathway, you'll need to configure an MX record for inbound email parsing. This allows Pathway to process incoming emails sent to your subdomain.

Add the following MX record to your DNS provider:

Field Value Description
Record Type MX Mail Exchange record for routing emails
Priority 10 Lower number = higher priority
Hostname mail Your subdomain prefix (e.g., mail for mail.yourdomain.com)
Value mail.pathwaymail.co Pathway's mail server

Inbound Email Address

Once configured, emails sent to *@mail.yourdomain.com will be parsed by Pathway. The wildcard (*) means any email address at your subdomain will be captured.

To verify your MX record:

  1. Add the MX record to your DNS provider (same process as TXT records)
  2. Wait for DNS propagation (usually a few minutes with short TTL)
  3. Click the Check MX Record button in Pathway to verify

Subdomain advantage

Since you're using a subdomain, adding an MX record won't affect your main domain's email routing. Your existing Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or other email services will continue to work normally.

Alternative: Use Inbox Routing Addresses

If you prefer not to modify your DNS at all for receiving emails, you can use Inbox Routing Addresses instead. This creates unique Pathway email addresses that forward to your inbox without changing your DNS.

How to set up routing addresses:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Practice → Communication → Email
  2. Scroll down to the Inbox Routing Addresses section
  3. Click Create Address to generate a unique routing email
  4. Select the routing address in the Reply-To Emails section

✓ No DNS changes required — Keep your existing email setup while still receiving patient replies through Pathway.

Important: Enable the Reply-To address

For inbox routing to work, you must select your routing address in the Reply-To Emails section. This ensures patient replies are directed to Pathway's inbox.
