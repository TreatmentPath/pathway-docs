Root Domain Setup Guide

Configure your root domain for sending emails through Pathway. This guide walks you through the complete DNS setup process.

Prerequisites

Domain ownership

You must own or have administrative access to the domain you want to configure.

DNS access

You need the ability to add TXT and CNAME records to your domain's DNS settings.

Pathway account

An active Pathway subscription with admin privileges.

Add Your Domain

Follow these steps to add your domain to Pathway. Once added, you'll receive the DNS records needed to complete the setup.

1

Open Settings

Locate the Settings icon (gear icon) in the sidebar on the left side of your screen. You'll find it near the bottom of the sidebar, just above the logout button. Click on it to open the Settings menu.

The Settings icon is highlighted in red in the bottom-left corner of the sidebar.

2

Navigate to Email Settings

Once in Settings, follow this navigation path to reach the Email Domains section:

  1. Click on the Practice tab in the top navigation bar
  2. Select the Communication tab from the secondary menu
  3. Click on the Email sub-tab to view email settings

The numbered boxes show the navigation sequence: (1) Practice tab, (2) Communication, (3) Email, and (4) the Add button.

3

Click "Add" to add your domain

In the Added Domains section, click the Add button (highlighted as step 4 in the screenshot above) to open the domain registration dialog.

4

Enter your domain name

Enter your domain (e.g., yourdomain.com) and click Register domain.

The dialog highlights where to enter your root domain and the Register domain button to confirm.

5

Review your DNS records

After registering your domain, you'll be taken to the domain configuration page. Here you'll see all the DNS records that need to be added to your domain provider. Your domain will show a pending status until verification is complete.

The domain configuration page displays all required DNS records. The highlighted areas show: (1) Hostname - the DNS record name, (2) Value - the record content to copy, and (3) Verify button - click after adding records.

This page displays four types of records:

  • TXT Verification Record — Proves you own this domain. Add this first.
  • TXT SPF Record — Authorizes Pathway to send emails on your behalf.
  • TXT DKIM Record — Adds a digital signature to authenticate your emails.
  • TXT DMARC Record — Protects your domain from email spoofing.

Scroll down to see the remaining DNS records — SPF, DKIM, and DMARC:

The remaining records: SPF (authorizes email sending), DKIM (cryptographic signature), and DMARC (spoofing protection). Each section shows the Record Type, Hostname, and Value you'll need to add to your DNS provider.

Tip: Use the copy button next to each value to copy it to your clipboard. Keep this page open while you configure your DNS settings.

6

Add DNS records to your domain provider

Log in to your domain registrar or DNS provider (e.g., Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap) and add each of the DNS records shown in Pathway. You'll need to create TXT records for verification, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. The example below shows how to add the verification record in Cloudflare.

Cloudflare DNS management interface for adding the verification TXT record. The numbered areas correspond to the steps below.

Follow these steps in Cloudflare (similar steps apply to other DNS providers):

1

Click "Records" in the sidebar

Under the DNS section in the left navigation, click on Records to view and manage your DNS records.

2

Select "TXT" as the record type

Click the Type dropdown and select TXT. All four records (verification, SPF, DKIM, DMARC) use TXT type.

3

Enter the Name (hostname)

For the verification record on a root domain, enter @ (which represents your domain). For other records like DKIM, enter the specific hostname shown in Pathway (e.g., treatmentpath._domainkey).

4

Paste the Content (value)

Copy the Value from Pathway and paste it into the Content field. For verification, this will look like treatmentpath-verify=... followed by a unique identifier.

5

Click "Save" to add the record

Once you've entered all the information, click the blue Save button to create the DNS record. Repeat this process for each of the four records.

Here are examples of adding the remaining DNS records in Cloudflare:

Adding the SPF Record:

For the SPF record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter @ for the name, (4) Paste the SPF value, and (5) Click Save.

Adding the DKIM Record:

For the DKIM record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter treatmentpath._domainkey for the name, (4) Paste the DKIM value, and (5) Click Save.

Adding the DMARC Record:

For the DMARC record: (1) Click Records, (2) Select TXT type, (3) Enter _dmarc for the name, (4) Paste the DMARC value, and (5) Click Save.

Recommended: Use the shortest TTL available

We recommend setting TTL (Time To Live) to the shortest value available — typically 60 seconds (1 minute) or 300 seconds (5 minutes). A shorter TTL allows DNS changes to propagate faster, making verification and troubleshooting quicker. In Cloudflare, you can leave it as Auto which uses a short TTL by default.

7

Verify your DNS records

After adding all DNS records, return to Pathway and click the Verify button next to each record. DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to propagate, so if verification fails initially, wait and try again later.

Once all your DNS records are properly configured, the verification page will show green checkmarks next to each record type (Verification Record, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC).

Successfully verified records display a green ✓ Verified badge next to the record name. Each record section shows the expected values that were found in your DNS configuration.

What each status means:

✓ Verified — Record found and correct
⏳ Pending — Waiting for DNS propagation

All verified?

Once all records show as verified, your domain is ready! You can now send emails from your custom domain through Pathway.

8

Set up Inbound Parse for receiving messages (Optional)

If you want to receive email replies from patients through Pathway, you'll need to configure an MX record for inbound email parsing. This allows Pathway to process incoming emails sent to your domain.

The Inbound Parse section shows the MX record details needed to receive emails. The numbered areas highlight the key fields.

Add the following MX record to your DNS provider:

Field Value Description
Record Type MX Mail Exchange record for routing emails
Priority 10 Lower number = higher priority
Hostname @ Root domain (use @ for the root)
Value mail.pathwaymail.co Pathway's mail server

Inbound Email Address

Once configured, emails sent to *@yourdomain.com will be parsed by Pathway. The wildcard (*) means any email address at your domain will be captured.

To verify your MX record:

  1. Add the MX record to your DNS provider (same process as TXT records)
  2. Wait for DNS propagation (usually a few minutes with short TTL)
  3. Click the Check MX Record button in Pathway to verify

Important: Existing MX Records

If you already have MX records (e.g., for Google Workspace or Microsoft 365), adding this record may affect your existing email routing. Consider using a subdomain for Pathway to avoid conflicts with your primary email service.

Alternative: Use Inbox Routing Addresses

If you don't want to modify your MX records (to keep Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or other email services intact), you can use Inbox Routing Addresses instead. This creates unique Pathway email addresses that forward to your inbox without changing your DNS.

The Inbox Routing Addresses section lets you create unique email addresses for routing messages without modifying MX records.

How to set up routing addresses:

  1. Navigate to Settings → Practice → Communication → Email
  2. Scroll down to the Inbox Routing Addresses section
  3. Click Create Address to generate a unique routing email
  4. Select the routing address in the Reply-To Emails section (see below)

✓ No DNS changes required — Keep your existing email setup while still receiving patient replies through Pathway.

Important: Enable the Reply-To address

For inbox routing to work, you must select your routing address in the Reply-To Emails section. This ensures patient replies are directed to Pathway's inbox.

Select your routing address in the Reply-To Emails section. The selected address will show an "Inbox" label indicating it's configured for routing.
