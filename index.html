Domain Setup Guide

Learn how to configure your custom domain for sending emails through TreatmentPath. This guide covers everything from adding your domain to configuring DNS records for optimal deliverability.

Before you begin

You'll need access to your domain's DNS settings. This is typically managed through your domain registrar (like GoDaddy, Namecheap) or DNS provider (like Cloudflare, Route 53).

What you'll set up

Verification Record

Prove ownership of your domain to TreatmentPath.

SPF Record

Authorize TreatmentPath to send emails on behalf of your domain.

DKIM Record

Cryptographically sign your emails to prevent tampering.

DMARC Record

Define how receiving servers handle authentication failures.

Prerequisites

Domain ownership

You must own or have administrative access to the domain you want to configure.

DNS access

You need the ability to add TXT and CNAME records to your domain's DNS settings.

TreatmentPath account

An active TreatmentPath subscription with admin privileges.

Add DNS records to your domain provider

Log in to your domain registrar or DNS provider (e.g., Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap) and add each of the DNS records shown in TreatmentPath. You'll need to create TXT records for verification, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. The example below shows how to add the verification record in Cloudflare.

Cloudflare DNS management interface for adding the verification TXT record. The numbered areas correspond to the steps below.

Follow these steps in Cloudflare (similar steps apply to other DNS providers):

1

Click "Records" in the sidebar

Under the DNS section in the left navigation, click on Records to view and manage your DNS records.

2

Select "TXT" as the record type

Click the Type dropdown and select TXT. All four records (verification, SPF, DKIM, DMARC) use TXT type.

3

Enter the Name (hostname)

For the verification record on a root domain, enter @ (which represents your domain). For other records like DKIM, enter the specific hostname shown in TreatmentPath (e.g., treatmentpath._domainkey).

4

Paste the Content (value)

Copy the Value from TreatmentPath and paste it into the Content field. For verification, this will look like treatmentpath-verify=... followed by a unique identifier.

5

Click "Save" to add the record

Once you've entered all the information, click the blue Save button to create the DNS record. Repeat this process for each of the four records.

Recommended: Use the shortest TTL available

We recommend setting TTL (Time To Live) to the shortest value available ‚Äî typically 60 seconds (1 minute) or 300 seconds (5 minutes). A shorter TTL allows DNS changes to propagate faster, making verification and troubleshooting quicker. In Cloudflare, you can leave it as Auto which uses a short TTL by default.

Set up Inbound Parse for receiving messages (Optional)

If you want to receive email replies from patients through TreatmentPath, you'll need to configure an MX record for inbound email parsing. This allows TreatmentPath to process incoming emails sent to your domain.

The Inbound Parse section shows the MX record details needed to receive emails. The numbered areas highlight the key fields.

Add the following MX record to your DNS provider:

Field Value Description
Record Type MX Mail Exchange record for routing emails
Priority 10 Lower number = higher priority
Hostname @ Root domain (or subdomain if using one)
Value mail.pathway.dental TreatmentPath's mail server

Inbound Email Address

Once configured, emails sent to *@yourdomain.com will be parsed by TreatmentPath. The wildcard (*) means any email address at your domain will be captured.

To verify your MX record:

  1. Add the MX record to your DNS provider (same process as TXT records)
  2. Wait for DNS propagation (usually a few minutes with short TTL)
  3. Click the Check MX Record button in TreatmentPath to verify

If you already have MX records (e.g., for Google Workspace or Microsoft 365), adding this record may affect your existing email routing. Consider using a subdomain for TreatmentPath to avoid conflicts with your primary email service.

Configure DNS Records

Add the following DNS records to your domain. The exact process varies by DNS provider - see provider-specific guides below.

DNS propagation can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours. Most changes propagate within 1-4 hours.

Required DNS Records

Type Host/Name Value TTL
TXT @ v=spf1 include:_spf.treatmentpath.com ~all 3600
CNAME tp._domainkey tp._domainkey.treatmentpath.com 3600
TXT _dmarc v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com 3600

Using a Subdomain

If you prefer to send emails from a subdomain (e.g., mail.yourdomain.com or notify.yourdomain.com), the DNS record hostnames will change slightly. This is recommended if you already have email services running on your main domain.

Why use a subdomain? Using a subdomain isolates your transactional emails from your main domain's reputation. If you already use Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, or another email provider on your root domain, a subdomain prevents conflicts.

Example: Subdomain mail.yourdomain.com

Record Type Host/Name Value
Verification TXT mail treatmentpath-verify=abc123...
SPF TXT mail v=spf1 include:treatmentpath.com ~all
DKIM TXT treatmentpath._domainkey.mail v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p=MIIBIjANBg...
DMARC TXT _dmarc.mail v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=100

Key differences for subdomains:

  • ‚úì Replace @ with your subdomain name (e.g., mail)
  • ‚úì For DKIM, prepend your subdomain: treatmentpath._domainkey.mail instead of treatmentpath._domainkey
  • ‚úì For DMARC, use _dmarc.mail instead of _dmarc
  • ‚úì Your sending address will be noreply@mail.yourdomain.com instead of noreply@yourdomain.com

DNS Provider Variations

Some DNS providers automatically append your domain to the hostname. If your provider does this, you may need to enter just mail instead of mail.yourdomain.com. Check your provider's documentation if records aren't resolving correctly.

SPF Record

Sender Policy Framework (SPF) is an email authentication method that specifies which mail servers are authorized to send email on behalf of your domain.

How to add the SPF record

TXT Record
v=spf1 include:_spf.treatmentpath.com ~all

Already have an SPF record?

If you already have an SPF record, don't create a new one. Instead, add our include statement to your existing record:

Before:

v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all

After:

v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com include:_spf.treatmentpath.com ~all

DKIM Record

DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM) adds a digital signature to your emails, allowing receiving servers to verify that the email hasn't been altered in transit.

How to add the DKIM record

CNAME Record

Host/Name: tp._domainkey

Points to: tp._domainkey.treatmentpath.com

Note: Some DNS providers require you to enter the full hostname including your domain (e.g., tp._domainkey.yourdomain.com), while others only need the subdomain part.

DMARC Record

Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance (DMARC) tells receiving servers what to do when an email fails SPF or DKIM checks.

Recommended DMARC policies

Recommended for starting
v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com

Monitor mode: Collects reports without affecting email delivery. Use this initially to understand your email ecosystem.

Intermediate
v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=10; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com

Quarantine mode: Sends 10% of failing emails to spam. Gradually increase the percentage.

Strict (after testing)
v=DMARC1; p=reject; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com

Reject mode: Rejects all emails that fail authentication. Only use after thorough testing.

Common Issues

DNS propagation can take time. Here's what to do:

  • Wait at least 15-30 minutes after adding records
  • Check using a DNS lookup tool like MXToolbox
  • Clear your local DNS cache
  • Try verifying again in a few hours

You can only have one SPF record per domain. Merge our include statement with your existing record:

v=spf1 include:existing.com include:_spf.treatmentpath.com ~all

Common DKIM issues:

  • Make sure you're using CNAME record type, not TXT
  • The hostname should be tp._domainkey, not the full domain
  • If using Cloudflare, ensure proxy is disabled (gray cloud)
  • Some providers add your domain automatically - don't include it twice

Even with proper authentication, emails might go to spam initially. Tips to improve deliverability:

  • Start with low volume and gradually increase
  • Ensure your email content isn't triggering spam filters
  • Keep your bounce rate low by using valid email addresses
  • Ask recipients to mark your emails as "not spam"
  • Consider warming up your domain over 2-4 weeks

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does domain verification take?

Once DNS records are properly configured, verification is instant. However, DNS propagation can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours, with most changes visible within 1-4 hours.

Can I use a subdomain instead of my main domain?

Yes, you can use a subdomain like mail.yourdomain.com. This is actually recommended if you have existing email services on your main domain.

Do I need all three records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)?

SPF and DKIM are required for good deliverability. DMARC is strongly recommended but optional initially. We recommend adding all three for the best email reputation.

Will this affect my existing email?

Adding TreatmentPath to your SPF record won't affect existing email services. Just make sure to merge our include statement with your existing SPF record rather than replacing it.

Can I add multiple domains?

Yes, you can add and verify multiple domains. Each domain will need its own set of DNS records configured.
